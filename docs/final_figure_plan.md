## Figure Logic

The final paper should read in this order:

1. define the pre-commit transport object
2. show that the productive region is a Pareto-like ridge with distinct tips
3. show that the ridge is created before final crossing by a falsifiable pre-commit principle
4. show what part of that principle survives beyond one geometry and beyond one metric

This means:

- Figure 3 is the main Results centerpiece
- Figure 4 is the scope figure that keeps the standing qualifier visible
- Figure 2 proves the numerical structure that Figure 3 interprets
- Figure 1 stops the paper from reading like a pure optimization study

## Figure 3

Figure 3 is the central evidence figure.

Title:

- Central evidence for the Pareto-like ridge and the pre-commit principle

Panels:

- `A` canonical ridge branches and the stale comparator in the `Pi_U`-`Pi_f` plane

Required takeaways:

- the decisive structure is already created before final crossing
- delay and flow act first on the timing budget
- memory acts more cleanly on approach-to-residence organization
- `commit_given_residence` is late and comparatively flat
- trap burden is a stale-edge flag, not the leading smooth control coordinate

## Figure 4

Required takeaways:

- what remains outside scope is visible rather than hidden

## What Was Removed Or Demoted

Removed from the main figures or demoted to extended data:

- full coarse-to-confirmatory localization chronology
- full `Pi_f`, `Pi_m`, and `Pi_U` profile sweeps as standalone main panels
- geometry-gallery panels that do not directly support the transfer verdict
- full metric-family comparison maps that exceed the one main scope point
- threshold-sensitivity and audit panels for the mechanism dataset

Reason:

- these panels are useful, but they no longer sit on the shortest path to the finalized ceiling claim

## Practical Rule For Main Text

Each main figure must answer one direct question.

- Figure 1: what is the transport object?
- Figure 2: what was discovered numerically?
- Figure 3: what mechanism and reduced principle explain it?
- Figure 4: how far does that principle safely travel?

If a panel cannot be assigned cleanly to one of those questions, it should move to extended data.

## Bottom Line

The final package is intentionally asymmetric:

- Figure 3 carries the main Results claim
- Figure 4 carries the scope qualifier
- Figures 1 and 2 prepare those two figures rather than competing with them

That is the layout that keeps the paper maximally strong without overselling beyond the tested family or beyond current bookkeeping.
