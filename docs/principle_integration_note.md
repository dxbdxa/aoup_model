## The Four-Package Storyline

The project has now completed four connected packages.

### Package 1: ridge discovery

What it established:

- the productive-memory window is not a single optimum but a Pareto-like ridge
- the ridge is narrow in delay, extended rather than point-like, and ordered mainly by flow
- success, efficiency, and speed separate into distinct front tips rather than collapsing to one winner

This moved the project beyond a one-metric optimization picture.

### Package 2: pre-commit gate theory

What it established:

- the decisive competitive structure is visible before final crossing
- the main transferable object is a pre-commit backbone from search to residence to commitment
- delay acts as an admissibility condition, memory acts as productive smoothing, and flow orders which branch of the backbone is favored

This moved the project beyond a descriptive ridge picture toward a mechanism-level principle.

### Package 3: geometry-tested universality

What it established:

- the backbone ordering survives the first geometry-transfer test across `GF0`, `GF1`, and `GF2`
- what transfers robustly is the shape-level ordering logic of the pre-commit backbone
- what renormalizes is the coefficient layer: geometric scales, encounter frequencies, and absolute rates

This moved the project beyond one gated maze.

### Package 4: thermodynamic upgrade

What it established:

- the Pareto-like ridge survives a more careful efficiency discussion
- `eta_sigma` remains useful for screening but is not the final thermodynamic answer
- `eta_completion_drag` is the strongest directly computed metric for manuscript-level thermodynamic discussion
- `eta_trap_drag` sharpens mechanism interpretation of stale pre-commit loss without changing the leading competitive branch

This moved the project beyond one optimization metric.

## Integrated Principle Statement

The strongest integrated principle statement is:

> Productive transport in delayed-memory guidance systems is organized by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered pre-commit backbone whose shape survives geometry transfer, while its coefficients renormalize and its thermodynamic reading becomes clearer under drag-normalized completion-aware metrics.

This integrated statement contains four layers at once:

- the structure is Pareto-like rather than single-optimum
- the decisive mechanism is pre-commit rather than final-crossing-first
- the transferable object is backbone shape rather than coefficient identity
- the thermodynamic upgrade changes branch preference along the ridge without destroying the ridge

## Strongest Current Claim

The strongest current project-level claim is:

- the productive-memory structure is a geometry-tested, Pareto-like pre-commit transport ridge
- this ridge is governed by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered commitment backbone
- under a directly computed thermodynamic upgrade, the ridge survives while the preferred efficient branch shifts toward the fast-completion branch

This is stronger than any one package alone because it combines front structure, reduced mechanism, first geometry transfer, and upgraded efficiency reading into one coherent transport statement.

## Strongest Safe Claim

The strongest safe claim is slightly narrower.

- the project has established a tested-family transport principle at the level of pre-commit backbone shape
- it has not yet established a full universal crossing law, a coefficient-exact collapse law, or a full total-entropy thermodynamic theory
- the most defensible thermodynamic statement is therefore that a compact, directly computed efficiency upgrade changes branch ordering along the ridge while preserving the ridge itself

This is the right language for manuscript-level framing because it keeps the principle strong without overreaching into unsupported closure.

## Most Important Open Extension

The most important open extension is a full completion-layer upgrade.

More explicitly, the next major extension is to connect the current pre-commit principle to:

- post-commit crossing structure
- fuller thermodynamic bookkeeping beyond drag-only cost
- a stronger geometry family that includes stress-test disorder or more irregular labyrinths

Among these, the single most important conceptual extension is:

- determine whether the same principle can be extended from a pre-commit backbone law to a completion-aware transport law without losing the current claim hierarchy

That is where the next real compression of the project would occur.

## How the project moved beyond one gated maze

The project is no longer just a result for one reference maze because the first geometry-transfer package changed the scope of the claim.

What is no longer maze-specific:

- the existence of a pre-commit backbone as the first competitive object
- the qualitative roles of delay, memory, and flow
- the distinction between shape-level universality and coefficient-level renormalization

What is still geometry-sensitive:

- exact scales
- exact encounter frequencies
- exact rate coefficients
- completion-layer details after commitment

So the project has moved beyond one gated maze at the level of principle shape, but not yet at the level of all coefficients and all completion phenomena.

## How the project moved beyond one optimization metric

The project is no longer tied to one efficiency metric because the thermodynamic-upgrade package showed that the ridge survives under metric refinement.

What changed:

- the preferred efficient branch shifts from the moderate-flow `eta_sigma` family to the high-flow `eta_completion_drag` branch

What did not change:

- the ridge remains Pareto-like
- the delay strip remains narrow
- the success front remains distinct
- the thermodynamic upgrade changes ordering along the ridge, not the existence of the ridge itself

That is exactly the right sense in which the project has moved beyond one metric: the principle survives the metric upgrade, even though the branch preference does not stay fixed.

## What is genuinely general here?

What is genuinely general at the current stage is not a universal coefficient law. It is a structural transport statement.

Genuinely general now:

- competitive transport is selected first on a pre-commit backbone
- delay has an admissibility role
- memory has a productive-smoothing versus stale-guidance role
- flow orders which competitive branch is favored
- the best current efficiency discussion should be tied to how productively the backbone converts drag spending into completed transport
- geometry changes coefficients before it destroys the backbone ordering logic

So the generality is at the level of transport organization and control roles.

## What remains outside the current principle?

The following remain outside the current principle and should stay explicit.

Outside scope now:

- full crossing-completion law
- coefficient-exact geometry collapse
- total entropy production
- full energetic accounting of propulsion, controller, memory-bath, and information costs
- universal claims for irregular stress-test geometries not yet run
- a final law for how efficiency, success, and speed must combine under all cost definitions

This boundary is not a weakness. It is what keeps the integrated principle scientifically credible.

## Bottom Line

The whole-project storyline is now coherent.

- first, the project discovered that productive transport forms a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum
- second, it explained that ridge through a pre-commit gate theory
- third, it showed that the backbone shape survives geometry transfer with coefficient renormalization
- fourth, it showed that a thermodynamic metric upgrade changes the preferred branch without destroying the ridge

The integrated result is therefore stronger than a one-maze optimization story and stronger than a one-metric ranking story. It is best described as a geometry-tested, thermodynamically upgraded, pre-commit transport principle with explicit scope limits.
