The paper argues that productive delayed-memory transport is not a single-maze optimization result but a geometry-tested, thermodynamically upgraded pre-commit transport principle: the system forms a Pareto-like ridge, that ridge is organized by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered backbone before final crossing, its shape survives first geometry transfer, and its branch ordering survives a conservative efficiency-metric upgrade even though the preferred efficient branch changes.

## Discussion Spine

The Discussion should answer four questions directly.

1. What was discovered quantitatively?
2. What mechanism explains it most robustly now?
3. What part of that mechanism generalizes beyond one geometry?
4. What part of the efficiency story is strengthened by the thermodynamic upgrade, and what remains open?

## Strongest Current Claim

- the productive-memory structure is a geometry-tested, Pareto-like pre-commit transport ridge
- the ridge is organized by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered commitment backbone

## Strongest Safe Claim

Use this level in the abstract, title selection, and final Discussion paragraph.

- the paper supports a geometry-tested pre-commit transport principle at the level of shape-level universality with coefficient-level renormalization
- it does not yet support a full crossing law, coefficient-exact universality, or full thermodynamic closure

## Future Extension

Use this level in the final Discussion and outlook.

- the main next extension is to connect the current pre-commit principle to post-commit completion structure and fuller thermodynamic bookkeeping without losing the present claim hierarchy

## Why this is more than a gated-maze optimization study

The manuscript should make this answer explicit.

- the central result is not one best operating point in one geometry
- the front structure is a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum
- the mechanism is stated as a pre-commit transport principle rather than as a descriptive optimization story
- the geometry-transfer package shows that the backbone shape survives beyond the reference maze
- the thermodynamic-upgrade package shows that the principle survives a metric upgrade rather than depending on one efficiency definition

That combination is what upgrades the work from a simulation optimization study to a principle paper.

## What is genuinely general here, and what remains outside scope?

Genuinely general now:

- pre-commit backbone organization as the first decisive transport object
- delay as admissibility, memory as productive smoothing versus stale guidance, and flow as branch-ordering coordinate
- shape-level universality with coefficient-level renormalization across the tested family
- survival of the Pareto-like ridge under the current thermodynamic metric upgrade

Outside scope now:

- full post-commit crossing law
- coefficient-exact universality across geometries
- total entropy production and full thermodynamic closure
- final claims for stronger disorder or untested stress-test geometries

## Final Paragraph Target

A strong final paragraph should say that the project has moved from locating a numerical productive window in one maze to establishing a geometry-tested, thermodynamically upgraded pre-commit transport principle with explicit scope limits.
