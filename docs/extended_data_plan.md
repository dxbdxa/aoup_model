## Demotion Principle

Move a panel to extended data if it does one of the following:

- supports a real claim, but not the shortest path to the ceiling claim
- repeats a main-text result at greater detail rather than higher leverage
- documents validation, audit, or chronology rather than carrying the main conceptual step
- is useful for specialists but not needed for title, abstract, or core Results compression

## What should stay out of both main figures and extended data claims

Do not use extended data to imply claims that remain unsupported.

Still outside claim scope:

- full crossing-completion law
- unrestricted geometry universality
- coefficient-exact universality
- full thermodynamic closure
- total entropy production
- a final branch winner invariant to fuller bookkeeping

Extended data may show why those claims remain open, but it should not quietly promote them.

## Bottom Line

Extended data should strengthen confidence, not expand the claim.

Main figures should carry:

- ridge discovery
- pre-commit principle
- tested-family transfer
- metric-robust ridge survival

Extended data should carry:

- chronology
- audit detail
- fuller slice detail
- family-specific comparisons
- bookkeeping and robustness detail beyond the main-text ceiling claim
