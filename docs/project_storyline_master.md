## Project Arc

The project begins as an optimization problem and ends, at the current stage, as a principle-discovery program.

Initial question:

- where does memory help gated active transport?

Current answer:

- memory helps only on a narrow productive ridge, and that ridge is best understood through a general pre-commit transport principle rather than through one best numerical optimum

## Stage 1: from scans to a ridge

The first breakthrough was numerical.

- coarse, refinement, precision, and confirmatory scans showed that the productive region is narrow and structured
- front analysis showed that the best success, efficiency, and speed points do not coincide
- the right geometric picture became a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single best point

This established the existence of a structured operating family.

## Stage 2: from a ridge to a principle

The second breakthrough was mechanistic.

- refined mechanism extraction and pre-commit theory showed that the decisive competition is visible before final crossing
- the key structure is a search-to-residence-to-commit backbone
- delay controls admissibility, memory controls productive smoothing versus staleness, and flow orders which branch of the backbone is favored

This established the first general transport principle candidate.

## Stage 3: from one geometry to tested-family generality

The third breakthrough was transfer.

- the first geometry-transfer validation showed that the pre-commit backbone ordering survives across the tested family
- the transferable object is the backbone shape, not exact coefficients and not yet full crossing success
- geometric scales and local coefficients renormalize, but the ordering logic persists

This moved the project beyond one gated maze.

## Stage 4: from one metric to upgraded efficiency language

The fourth breakthrough was interpretive.

- the thermodynamic bookkeeping note clarified that `eta_sigma` is useful but incomplete
- the metric-upgrade package showed that the Pareto-like ridge survives better-separated cost accounting
- the main change is that the preferred efficient branch shifts toward the fast-completion branch under `eta_completion_drag`
- trap-aware refinement does not overturn that shift

This moved the project beyond one optimization metric.

## Master claim

The master claim is:

> Productive transport in delayed-memory guidance systems is organized by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered pre-commit backbone. This backbone generates a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum, survives the first geometry-transfer test at the level of shape, and retains that ridge structure under a conservative thermodynamic metric upgrade even though the preferred efficient branch changes.

## What is genuinely general here?

General now:

- the ridge is a structural family, not a one-point optimum
- the decisive mechanism is pre-commit backbone organization
- the roles of delay, memory, and flow are stable under the first geometry transfer
- the principle survives a metric upgrade rather than depending on one numerical efficiency definition

This is why the project has become a control-law discovery story rather than a single parameter-scan result.

## What remains outside the current principle?

Still outside scope:

- full post-commit completion theory
- full entropy-production accounting
- coefficient-exact universality
- final claims for stronger disorder or stress-test geometries

These are the main extensions that would move the project from a tested-family principle to a broader transport law.

## Manuscript structure suggestion

A compact manuscript storyline can now be organized as follows.

1. Define the gated transport problem and reference scales.
2. Show the productive-memory ridge and the distinct Pareto-like front tips.
3. Explain the ridge through the pre-commit backbone principle.
4. Show that the backbone shape survives geometry transfer.
5. Show that the ridge also survives a thermodynamic metric upgrade, even though branch preference changes.
6. State the integrated principle and its explicit scope limits.

## Strongest safe ending sentence

A good closing sentence for the current project is:

> The project has moved from identifying a numerical productive window in one maze to establishing a geometry-tested, thermodynamically upgraded pre-commit transport principle with explicit scope limits.
