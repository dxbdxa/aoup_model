## The Reset

The paper should no longer read as:

- an optimization campaign that found a productive parameter window in one gated maze
- a specialist workflow story built around scan stages, metric choices, and reduced-model machinery

The paper should instead read as:

- a physics paper about when transport performance is actually selected in constrained non-equilibrium transport
- a discovery paper in which a broad question is answered by identifying a single organizing object and then testing that object against transfer and metric-upgrade challenges

The opening conceptual contrast should be simple:

- one possible view is that transport performance is chosen at the final barrier-crossing event
- the alternative view is that performance is selected earlier, by a pre-commit dynamical organization that already fixes the tradeoff between reliability, speed, and cost

The present work supports the second view, with explicit scope limits.

## Central Scientific Question

The main scientific question is:

> In constrained non-equilibrium transport, is performance selected at the moment of final barrier crossing, or is it determined earlier by a pre-commit dynamical organization that trades off reliability, speed, and cost?

This is the question that should appear in the title logic, abstract logic, introduction, and discussion. The paper should keep returning to it.

## Main Principle To Preserve

The main principle to preserve is:

> Across the tested family and within current bookkeeping, productive delayed-memory transport is organized by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered pre-commit backbone that produces a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum.

Geometry transfer and thermodynamic upgrade should be presented as principle-strengthening tests of this statement, not as separate stories with equal narrative weight.

## What is the one-sentence intellectual advance?

Across the tested family and within current bookkeeping, the paper shows that transport performance is selected primarily before final crossing, by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered pre-commit backbone that generates a Pareto-like ridge of reliability, speed, and cost tradeoffs rather than a single optimum.

## What makes this more than a gated-maze optimization study?

It becomes more than a gated-maze optimization study because the paper is no longer mainly about which parameter tuple wins in one layout. It is about a more general physical question: when a constrained non-equilibrium transport system decides whether it will be reliable, fast, or efficient.

The work clears that bar because it delivers four things in sequence:

- a structural discovery: performance lives on a Pareto-like ridge, not at one optimum
- a mechanistic discovery: that ridge is organized before final crossing by a pre-commit backbone
- a transfer test: the first transferable object is the backbone shape, while coefficients renormalize
- a robustness test: the ridge survives a thermodynamic metric upgrade even though the preferred efficient branch shifts

That combination is the manuscript's real value. The maze is the concrete platform. The paper's claim is about transport selection.

## The Narrative Spine

The main text should unfold as one continuous answer to the central question.

### Act I: pose the physical question

Start from the broad transport puzzle:

- in many constrained transport problems, the most visible event is the final barrier crossing
- but visibility is not the same as selection
- the unresolved issue is whether the decisive performance sorting occurs at that last step or earlier in the search-to-commit dynamics

Only after that question is on the table should the paper introduce this delayed-memory gated transport system as a clean testbed.

### Act II: establish the phenomenon that needs explanation

Show first that the system does not select one best operating point.

- productive transport occupies a narrow Pareto-like ridge
- success, speed, and efficiency separate into distinct front tips
- the ridge is narrow in delay and ordered mainly by flow

This creates the explanatory target.

### Act III: answer the question mechanistically

The central mechanistic result is that the competitive structure is already visible before final crossing.

- the decisive object is a pre-commit backbone linking search, wall-guided search, gate-local residence, and commitment
- delay determines whether that backbone remains admissible
- memory smooths the backbone when it is productive and makes it stale when it is not
- flow orders which branch of the admissible backbone is favored

This is the point where the paper should give the reader the single new physical object to remember: the pre-commit backbone.

### Act IV: show the principle is not one-geometry numerology

Geometry transfer comes next as a strengthening test.

- what survives is backbone shape and ordering logic
- what changes is the coefficient layer
- this means the paper is not claiming a coefficient-exact collapse or a universal completion law

The geometry section should therefore read as: the principle survives at the level that matters first.

### Act V: show the principle is not tied to one efficiency metric

Thermodynamic upgrade is the second strengthening test.

- the ridge survives the upgrade
- the preferred efficient branch changes
- the principle therefore does not depend on one screening metric, even though full closure is still open

This section should reinforce the same message: the structural principle survives a better physical reading.

### Act VI: end with a scoped principle, not an inflated law

The ending should state clearly what has been established and what has not.

- established: a tested-family pre-commit transport principle with a Pareto-like ridge
- not established: a full crossing-completion law, unrestricted universality, coefficient-exact geometry collapse, or full thermodynamic closure

That scope discipline is part of the paper's strength.

## Editorial Rules For The Main Text

Lead with:

- the physical question about when performance is selected
- the ridge as the phenomenon
- the pre-commit backbone as the answer
- geometry transfer and thermodynamic upgrade as tests of that answer

Do not lead with:

- scan chronology
- optimization workflow
- metric bookkeeping details
- specialist state-labeling before the reader knows why the distinction matters

Translate specialist language into broad-reader language whenever possible:

- "pre-commit backbone" should be defined in plain words the first time it appears
- "Pareto-like ridge" should be introduced as an extended family of tradeoff states, not as optimization jargon
- "geometry transfer" should be framed as a test of whether the organizing object survives a change of geometry
- "thermodynamic upgrade" should be framed as a test of whether the story survives improved cost accounting

## Main Discoveries In Priority Order

The paper should clearly signal this ranking of discoveries:

1. The central discovery is that productive transport forms a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum.
2. The main mechanistic discovery is that this ridge is selected before final crossing by a pre-commit backbone.
3. The first principle-strengthening discovery is that the backbone survives geometry transfer at the level of shape, while coefficients renormalize.
4. The second principle-strengthening discovery is that the ridge survives thermodynamic upgrade even though the preferred efficient branch shifts.

This ranking prevents geometry transfer and thermodynamic analysis from becoming competing side stories.

## What Belongs In Main Text

Keep in the main text:

- the central question
- the ridge discovery
- the pre-commit backbone and the key observables that make early selection legible
- one compact geometry-transfer section focused on shape survival and renormalization
- one compact thermodynamic-upgrade section focused on ridge survival and branch reordering
- the explicit scope limits

## What Belongs In Supporting Information

Move to Supporting Information:

- scan chronology and pipeline details
- full uncertainty tables and alternate front projections
- event definitions, thresholding, and fitting details
- full transition tables and state-graph construction details
- geometry-specific calibration details and any local renormalization slices
- metric derivations, proxy definitions, and bookkeeping caveats in full detail

The Supporting Information should defend the claim hierarchy, not compete with the main story.

## Explicit Exclusions That Must Stay Visible

The manuscript must preserve these exclusions explicitly:

- not a full crossing-completion law
- not unrestricted universality
- not coefficient-exact geometry collapse
- not full thermodynamic closure

These exclusions should appear in the Discussion and should also be visible in the abstract or title logic through phrasing such as:

- across the tested family
- within current bookkeeping
- at the level of backbone shape

## Bottom Line

The paper should read as a broad transport-selection paper built around one answer:

- performance is not first selected at the final barrier crossing
- it is selected earlier by a pre-commit dynamical backbone
- that backbone creates a Pareto-like ridge
- and that principle survives the first geometry-transfer and thermodynamic-upgrade tests within explicit scope limits
