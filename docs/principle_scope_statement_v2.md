## Strongest Safe Principle Statement

The strongest safe principle statement is:

> Across the currently tested geometry family and within the current bookkeeping, productive delayed-memory transport is organized by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered pre-commit backbone that produces a Pareto-like competitive ridge rather than a single optimum. The backbone survives geometry transfer at the level of shape, while coefficients renormalize, and the ridge survives the current thermodynamic metric upgrade even though the preferred efficient branch changes.

This statement is the safest project-level compression because every component is already supported by completed evidence, but each component also retains an explicit scope qualifier.

## Geometry-Tested Principle

What is meant here by a geometry-tested principle:

- the principle has been checked on the completed first family `GF0`, `GF1`, and `GF2`
- the transferable object is the pre-commit backbone rather than full crossing success
- the tested invariant is shape-level ordering logic, not coefficient identity
- the correct current transfer reading is shape-level survival plus coefficient-level renormalization

This is stronger than a one-maze claim and weaker than unrestricted universality.

## Geometry-Robust Candidate

A geometry-robust candidate is the next broader claim one might reasonably discuss, but only with care.

Current geometry-robust candidate:

- the same pre-commit backbone principle may organize a wider class of gated search-and-commit geometries beyond `GF0-GF2`

Why it is not yet the strongest safe claim:

- irregular stress-test geometry remains deferred
- coefficient renormalization is only calibrated on the present small family
- crossing-completion structure has not yet been shown to transfer even when backbone shape does

So geometry-robust language is acceptable in Discussion as a candidate extension, but not as an unrestricted statement of present proof.

## Unrestricted Universality Claim

An unrestricted universality claim would mean something stronger than the project currently supports.

That stronger claim would require all of the following:

- stable survival of the same backbone ordering on a broader and harder geometry family, including stress-test irregular labyrinths
- coefficient behavior that is either quantitatively collapsible or at least predictably renormalizable across that broader family
- evidence that post-commit completion does not overturn the current pre-commit hierarchy
- stronger exclusion of alternative reduced principles

That level has not yet been reached.

## Thermodynamic Scope

The thermodynamic scope should be stated separately rather than merged silently into the principle statement.

### Rigorous now

- the model explicitly computes a drag-dissipation proxy
- `eta_sigma` is a rigorous model-internal screening metric
- `eta_completion_drag` is a directly computed metric using current observables
- `eta_trap_drag` is a transparent stale-loss proxy refinement
- the Pareto-like ridge survives under the current metric upgrade
- the preferred efficient branch changes under the upgrade, but the ridge itself does not collapse

### Proxy-based now

- the current thermodynamic reading still depends partly on proxy quantities
- drag is explicitly booked, but controller burden, stale-loss burden, and completion-layer costs are only partially represented or indirectly inferred
- `eta_trap_drag` is a mechanism-sensitive proxy, not a first-principles thermodynamic closure

### Outside closure now

- total entropy production
- complete energetic bookkeeping for active propulsion
- controller actuation work
- information-rate or update-cost terms
- a full pre-commit versus post-commit cost decomposition
- a final thermodynamic efficiency law for the tested family, let alone for a broader one

## What this project does not yet claim

The project does not yet claim any of the following.

- a full crossing-completion law
- a universal crossing law across geometries
- coefficient-exact universality
- full total entropy production
- full thermodynamic closure
- proof that the same principle survives irregular stress-test geometries
- proof that the thermodynamic branch ordering is invariant under fuller bookkeeping

These exclusions should remain visible wherever the principle statement is used in title, abstract, Results, or Discussion.

## What would be needed to justify a stronger universality claim

A stronger universality claim would require new decisive results, not just stronger wording.

Needed evidence:

- completed transfer on stress-test geometries such as `GF3_RANDOM_LABYRINTH_STRESS_TEST`
- demonstration that the same backbone ordering survives beyond the current clean family
- evidence that coefficient renormalization remains interpretable rather than arbitrary across the broader family
- some stronger handle on the completion layer, showing it does not overturn the current backbone claim
- a clearer exclusion test against alternative reduced principles

Only after those results would it be justified to move from `geometry-tested principle` toward a broader `geometry-robust principle` in the main claim.

## What would be needed to justify a stronger thermodynamic claim

A stronger thermodynamic claim would also require new decisive results.

Needed evidence:

- explicit bookkeeping for active propulsion work
- explicit bookkeeping for controller or steering work
- explicit bookkeeping for information-rate or update costs if those are to be discussed thermodynamically
- a cleaner separation of pre-commit and post-commit costs
- evidence that the ridge structure and branch ordering survive after those missing channels are added
- justification that any resulting efficiency quantity is more than a useful proxy and can be read as a genuine thermodynamic closure

Until then, the thermodynamic language should remain tiered and conservative.

## Practical Language Rule

The safest standing qualifier is:

- across the tested family and within current bookkeeping

If a sentence would become misleading after removing that qualifier, then it is not yet a safe project-level principle statement.

## Bottom Line

The project currently supports a geometry-tested, thermodynamically upgraded pre-commit transport principle with explicit scope limits.

That means:

- stronger than a one-maze optimization result
- stronger than a one-metric efficiency story
- not yet a full universality law
- not yet a full thermodynamic closure
