## Lead Abstract-Safe Claim

Use this as the abstract-level ceiling:

> Across the tested family and within current bookkeeping, productive delayed-memory transport is organized by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered pre-commit backbone that forms a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum. Backbone shape survives geometry transfer while coefficients renormalize, and the ridge survives metric upgrade even though the preferred efficient branch changes.

This is the strongest safe abstract claim because it includes:

- structural discovery
- pre-commit mechanism
- tested-family transfer
- thermodynamic-upgrade survival
- the standing scope qualifier

## Abstract-Safe Sentence Set

Use the abstract in this order.

Opening sentence:

> Productive delayed-memory transport is not organized by a single best operating point, but by a Pareto-like ridge with distinct success, efficiency, and speed branches.

Mechanism sentence:

> The decisive competitive structure is already created before final crossing by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered pre-commit backbone linking search, residence, and commitment.

Transfer sentence:

> Across GF0, GF1, and GF2, this backbone transfers at the level of shape while geometric scales and local coefficients renormalize.

Thermodynamic-upgrade sentence:

> Within current bookkeeping, the ridge also survives metric refinement, even though the preferred efficient branch shifts from the moderate-flow screening family to the high-flow completion-aware family.

Closing scope sentence:

> The strongest safe reading is therefore a geometry-tested pre-commit transport principle across the tested family and within current bookkeeping, not a full crossing law or a full thermodynamic closure.

## What belongs in the abstract, and what belongs only in the discussion

Belongs in the abstract:

- Pareto-like ridge rather than single optimum
- distinct success, efficiency, and speed branches
- pre-commit backbone as the first decisive transport object
- delay, memory, and flow as the three organizing control roles
- shape-level transfer across GF0/GF1/GF2 with coefficient renormalization
- ridge survival under the current metric family
- explicit statement that branch preference changes under the upgrade
- the standing qualifier `across the tested family and within current bookkeeping`

Belongs only in the discussion:

- possible extension to a full completion-layer law
- possible extension to GF3 or broader stress-test geometries
- full thermodynamic closure or total entropy production
- any claim that the final efficient branch is invariant to fuller bookkeeping
- any claim of coefficient-exact universality
- any claim that one universal scalar isolates success everywhere
- any claim that trap burden is already a robust universal stale-control diagnostic

## Phrases That Are Safe Versus Unsafe

Safe:

- `geometry-tested pre-commit transport principle`
- `shape-level transfer with coefficient renormalization`
- `ridge survival under metric upgrade`
- `across the tested family and within current bookkeeping`

Unsafe for abstract use:

- `universal transport law`
- `full thermodynamic efficiency`
- `total entropy production`
- `geometry-independent coefficients`
- `crossing-completion universality`
- `final efficiency law`

## Short Abstract Closing Options

Most complete:

> These results support a geometry-tested, thermodynamically upgraded pre-commit transport principle across the tested family and within current bookkeeping.

More conservative:

> These results support a tested-family pre-commit transport principle with explicit geometry and thermodynamic scope limits.

Fallback if space is tight:

> The strongest safe conclusion is a geometry-tested pre-commit principle, not a one-maze optimum.

## Bottom Line

The abstract should sound strong because the evidence stack is now broad, but it should still remain disciplined:

- say `principle`, not `universal law`
- say `shape transfer`, not `full transport transfer`
- say `metric upgrade`, not `thermodynamic closure`
- carry the standing qualifier whenever the sentence compresses more than one evidence package
