## Overall Design

The main text should be short, question-driven, and cumulative.

Recommended structure:

1. Introduction framed by the selection-timing question
2. Results section establishing the Pareto-like ridge
3. Results section showing that selection occurs before final crossing
4. Results section showing that the transferable object is backbone shape
5. Results section showing that the principle survives thermodynamic upgrade
6. Discussion stating the scoped principle and explicit exclusions

The scan workflow should not define the section order.

## Title And Abstract Logic

The title and abstract should answer a broad question, not advertise a specialist pipeline.

Title logic:

- emphasize when transport performance is selected
- name pre-commit organization or pre-commit backbone
- name Pareto-like tradeoffs or ridge behavior
- avoid titles built around "optimization," "active-memory control," or scan nomenclature

Abstract logic:

- first sentence: broad transport-selection question
- second sentence: this system as a clean testbed
- third sentence: discovery of a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum
- fourth sentence: evidence that the ridge is selected before final crossing by a pre-commit backbone
- fifth sentence: geometry transfer and thermodynamic upgrade strengthen the same principle
- closing sentence: explicit scope limit

## Section 1: Introduction

### Working title

When is transport performance selected?

### Core job

The introduction should frame the long-standing physical issue:

- many constrained transport systems culminate in a final barrier-crossing event
- it is often unclear whether that event creates performance or merely reveals earlier selection
- the paper tests that distinction in a controlled delayed-memory transport system

### What the introduction must establish

- why selection timing is a broad non-equilibrium transport question
- why reliability, speed, and cost form the relevant competing performance axes
- why the current system is a useful testbed for separating pre-commit organization from final crossing

### What to keep out

- scan chronology
- detailed metric definitions
- specialist reduced-theory notation
- extended geometry taxonomy

### Introduction endpoint

The introduction should end by telling the reader exactly what will be shown:

- performance occupies a Pareto-like ridge
- that ridge is selected before final crossing
- the organizing backbone survives tested-family transfer and current metric upgrade

## Section 2: Productive transport forms a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum

### Core claim

The first result should establish the phenomenon to be explained:

- the productive region is an extended Pareto-like ridge
- the ridge is narrow in delay
- success, efficiency, and speed lie on distinct tips
- flow orders the tradeoff along the ridge

### What to show

- one compact system schematic and nondimensional control definition
- the ridge in the main control plane
- the non-dominated set and distinct front tips
- the narrow `Pi_f` support and dominant `Pi_U` ordering

### Reader takeaway

The system does not present one best operating point. It presents a structured family of choices. That creates the need for a selection principle.

### Keep in SI

- coarse, refinement, precision, and confirmatory scan history
- full uncertainty tables
- extra front projections
- overlap tables and candidate inventories in full

## Section 3: Selection occurs before final crossing

### Core claim

The second result should answer the manuscript's main question:

- the decisive competitive structure is visible before final crossing
- a pre-commit backbone organizes the ridge

### What to show

- the coarse-grained pre-commit state graph in intuitive form
- the canonical operating points
- the pre-commit observables that separate the branches
- the balanced-ridge versus stale-control comparison

### Required mechanistic message

Make three ideas legible to a broad reader:

- delay is an admissibility condition for the backbone
- memory is productive only when it smooths without going stale
- flow orders which branch of the admissible backbone is favored

### What not to overstate

- do not claim a full doorway-crossing law
- do not imply that post-commit dynamics are irrelevant
- do not imply that efficiency is fully derived from the current reduced theory

### Keep in SI

- full transition tables
- event definitions and thresholds
- fit reports and alternative graph granularities
- sparse crossing-branch evidence

## Section 4: The transferable object is backbone shape, not exact coefficients

### Core claim

The third result should show that the paper has moved beyond one geometry, but only at the right level:

- the pre-commit backbone survives across the tested family
- what transfers is shape and ordering logic
- what renormalizes is the coefficient layer

### What to show

- a simple three-geometry comparison
- the survival of the same backbone logic across `GF0`, `GF1`, and `GF2`
- the corresponding coefficient shifts or renormalization summary

### Reader takeaway

The paper's main claim is not tied to one layout, but it is also not claiming full geometry-independent collapse.

### Language rule

Use phrasing such as:

- across the tested family
- at the level of backbone shape
- with coefficient renormalization

Avoid phrasing such as:

- universal law across geometries
- geometry-independent coefficients
- full completion transfer

### Keep in SI

- geometry specifications
- replay protocols
- local renormalization slices
- calibration details
- deferred stress-test plans

## Section 5: The ridge survives thermodynamic upgrade even though the preferred efficient branch changes

### Core claim

The fourth result should show that the principle is not an artifact of one screening metric:

- the ridge survives a more physical metric upgrade
- the preferred efficient branch changes
- the structural principle does not collapse

### What to show

- `eta_sigma` as the screening metric
- `eta_completion_drag` as the strongest directly computed manuscript-level upgrade
- `eta_trap_drag` as a mechanism-facing refinement
- ridge survival and branch reordering under the upgrade

### Reader takeaway

Improved cost accounting changes which branch looks best, but it does not change the deeper answer to when performance is selected.

### What not to overstate

- do not claim full thermodynamic closure
- do not claim total entropy production
- do not present the current metric family as final

### Keep in SI

- full metric definitions
- bookkeeping derivations
- proxy caveats
- extended ranking tables and alternative presentations

## Section 6: Discussion

### Working title

A scoped principle for constrained non-equilibrium transport

### Core job

The Discussion should synthesize the paper in one sentence, broaden the significance carefully, and state the boundaries explicitly.

### What the Discussion should do

- restate the answer to the selection-timing question
- name the Pareto-like ridge and pre-commit backbone as the central discoveries
- explain why geometry transfer and thermodynamic upgrade strengthen the principle
- say clearly what remains open

### Explicit exclusions that must appear

- not a full crossing-completion law
- not unrestricted universality
- not coefficient-exact geometry collapse
- not full thermodynamic closure

### Discussion-only forward look

These belong only here, not in the Results as current claims:

- extension to a full completion-aware transport law
- stronger geometry families including irregular stress tests
- fuller thermodynamic bookkeeping

## Main Text Compression Rules

If space is tight, protect these items first:

1. The question about when performance is selected
2. The Pareto-like ridge
3. The pre-commit backbone
4. Shape-level geometry transfer
5. Thermodynamic-upgrade survival
6. The explicit exclusions

Compress these items before cutting the core story:

- scan history
- parameter-search logistics
- alternate proxy metrics
- fine-grained fit details
- extensive robustness appendices

## Main Text One-Paragraph Summary

The main text should tell one story in one line of logic: constrained transport does not choose one best state at the final crossing event; instead, across the tested family and within current bookkeeping, it is selected earlier by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered pre-commit backbone that generates a Pareto-like ridge of reliability, speed, and cost tradeoffs.
