## Level 1: strongest current claim

- the productive-memory structure is a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum
- the ridge is organized by a pre-commit commitment backbone
- the backbone is delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, and flow-ordered
- the backbone shape survives the first geometry-transfer test across the tested family
- the ridge survives the thermodynamic upgrade even though the preferred efficient branch changes

## Level 2: strongest safe claim

- the project supports a geometry-tested pre-commit transport principle at the level of shape-level universality
- the project supports coefficient-level renormalization rather than coefficient invariance
- the project supports a compact thermodynamic-upgrade discussion based on directly computed drag-normalized metrics
- the project does not yet support full total-entropy or full completion-law claims

## Level 3: useful but still proxy-based claim

- `eta_sigma` is useful for screening but limited
- `eta_completion_drag` is the strongest directly computed metric for manuscript thermodynamic discussion
- `eta_trap_drag` is useful for mechanism interpretation of stale pre-commit loss
- these metrics support a stronger efficiency discussion without constituting a complete thermodynamic closure

## Level 4: open extension that is strongly motivated

- extend the current pre-commit principle into a fuller completion-layer theory
- separate pre-commit and post-commit thermodynamic costs more explicitly
- test stronger geometry families, especially stress-test disorder
- determine whether a broader geometry family still preserves the same shape-level principle

## What is genuinely general here?

General now:

- the ordering roles of delay, memory, and flow
- the pre-commit backbone as the first decisive transport object
- the distinction between shape-level universality and coefficient-level renormalization
- the persistence of a Pareto-like ridge under a thermodynamic metric upgrade

Not general yet:

- exact coefficients
- full crossing laws
- full thermodynamic closure
- full stress-test geometry universality

## What remains outside the current principle?

Outside scope now:

- total entropy production
- complete active-work and controller-work accounting
- universal post-commit completion law
- coefficient-exact master collapse across all geometries
- final claims for untested stress-test geometries

## Recommended one-sentence hierarchy summary

> The project currently supports a geometry-tested, thermodynamically upgraded pre-commit transport principle at the level of structural backbone ordering, while leaving full completion-law, full thermodynamic closure, and coefficient-exact universality as open extensions.
