## Strongest Portable Claim

The strongest portable project-level claim is:

> Across the tested family and within current bookkeeping, productive delayed-memory transport is organized by a delay-admissible, memory-smoothed, flow-ordered pre-commit backbone that generates a Pareto-like ridge rather than a single optimum; backbone shape transfers while coefficients renormalize, and the ridge survives metric upgrade even though branch preference changes.

This is the ceiling claim for the paper. Everything shorter should compress this statement without silently expanding its scope.

## Narrative Ladder

Use this five-step ladder whenever the paper needs to move from data to principle.

1. Numerical discovery: productive operation is a Pareto-like ridge, not a single optimum, and success, efficiency, and speed occupy distinct front tips.
2. Mechanism: the ridge is already created before final crossing by differences in pre-commit timing, residence organization, and recycling burden.
3. Reduced principle: delay acts as admissibility, memory acts as productive smoothing versus stale guidance, and flow orders which branch of the pre-commit backbone is favored.
4. Geometry-tested scope: across GF0, GF1, and GF2, backbone shape survives while coefficients renormalize, so the transferable object is structural ordering rather than coefficient identity.
5. Thermodynamic-upgrade scope: within current bookkeeping, the ridge survives metric refinement even though the preferred efficient branch shifts from the moderate-flow screening family to the high-flow completion-aware family.

## Claims That Must Stay Out Of Title And Abstract

The following statements are still too broad for title or abstract use:

- full crossing-completion law
- unrestricted universality across arbitrary or irregular geometries
- coefficient-exact geometry collapse
- full thermodynamic closure
- total entropy production
- a final branch winner that is invariant to fuller bookkeeping
- any claim that GF3 or stronger stress-test geometries already support the same principle
- any claim that one unique scalar isolates success across the tested family
- any claim that trap burden is already a universal stale-control invariant

## Title Rule

The title should carry the shortest safe noun phrase, not the full evidence stack.

Preferred title-level compression:

- `A geometry-tested pre-commit transport principle for delayed-memory gated transport`

Avoid title phrases such as:

- `universal transport law`
- `full thermodynamic transport principle`
- `geometry-independent transport coefficients`
- `crossing-completion law`

## Abstract Rule

The abstract may integrate all four completed packages, but it should do so in the following order:

1. ridge discovery
2. pre-commit mechanism
3. geometry-tested transfer
4. metric-robust thermodynamic upgrade
5. explicit scope limit

If one clause must be dropped for length, drop detail before dropping scope. The scope sentence is what keeps the abstract scientifically safe.

## If this were rejected for being too broad, what wording would be narrowed first?

Narrow in this order:

1. Remove `thermodynamically upgraded` before removing `geometry-tested`.
2. Replace `principle` with `tested-family transport organizing rule` if the editor resists principle language.
3. Replace `geometry-tested` with `shape-tested across GF0/GF1/GF2` if the transfer claim is judged too global.
4. Move branch-reordering language from title or abstract into Results if the thermodynamic upgrade feels too expansive.
5. Replace `gated transport` with `gated delayed-memory transport in the tested family` if even the title feels too portable.

What should not be narrowed first:

- do not drop the pre-commit qualifier before dropping thermodynamic language
- do not drop the tested-family qualifier before dropping universality-flavored nouns
- do not strengthen the thermodynamic claim to compensate for a narrower geometry claim

## Bottom Line

The best editorial stance is:

- title: geometry-tested pre-commit principle
- abstract: integrated four-package claim with the standing qualifier
- Results: full evidence ladder
- Discussion: strongest integrated meaning plus explicit exclusions

That keeps the paper maximally strong without promoting any out-of-scope statement into the title or abstract.
