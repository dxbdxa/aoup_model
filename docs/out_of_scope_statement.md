## Out-of-Scope Summary

The current project should not be described as if it had already established:

- a full crossing-completion theory
- unrestricted universality across arbitrary gated geometries
- coefficient-exact geometry collapse
- full total-entropy production
- complete thermodynamic efficiency

Those statements go beyond the completed evidence.

## What this project does not yet claim

Not claimed now:

- that the completion layer obeys the same principle with no new bottlenecks
- that all irregular or strongly disordered geometries preserve the same backbone ordering
- that geometry changes only through simple coefficient rescaling under all conditions
- that the current metric family closes the full cost accounting
- that `eta_completion_drag` is the final thermodynamic efficiency rather than the best directly computed current upgrade
- that the current tested-family result automatically extends to a broader universality class

## Geometry Scope Boundary

Inside scope now:

- `GF0_REF_NESTED_MAZE`
- `GF1_SINGLE_BOTTLENECK_CHANNEL`
- `GF2_PORE_ARRAY_STRIP`
- shape-level backbone transfer with coefficient renormalization

Outside scope now:

- `GF3_RANDOM_LABYRINTH_STRESS_TEST`
- unrestricted generality across disordered or topologically harsher families
- any claim that full success or completion-law structure has already transferred

## Thermodynamic Scope Boundary

Inside scope now:

- drag-dissipation proxy bookkeeping
- `eta_sigma` for screening
- `eta_completion_drag` for direct upgraded discussion
- `eta_trap_drag` for mechanism interpretation
- ridge survival under current metric upgrade

Outside closure now:

- total entropy production
- active-fuel or propulsion work bookkeeping
- controller work bookkeeping
- information or update-cost bookkeeping
- full pre-commit versus post-commit cost decomposition
- final thermodynamic completion law

## What would be needed to justify a stronger universality claim

Needed:

- completed stress-test geometry evidence
- broader transfer family with stable shape-level survival
- clearer renormalization behavior across that broader family
- evidence that the completion layer does not overturn the current pre-commit hierarchy

## What would be needed to justify a stronger thermodynamic claim

Needed:

- explicit missing cost channels added to bookkeeping
- completion-layer cost separation
- stability of ridge and branch ordering under fuller cost accounting
- evidence that the resulting efficiency quantity can be read as more than a proxy

## Editorial Use Rule

If a sentence implies any of the following, it should be revised or moved to Discussion-only speculation:

- universal crossing law
- full thermodynamic closure
- geometry-independent coefficients
- proof beyond the tested family

## Bottom Line

The current project has a strong scoped principle, not an unrestricted law. Its power comes from making that scope explicit rather than hiding it.
